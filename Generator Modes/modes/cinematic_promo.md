# Cinematic Promo / Branded Story
**Mode ID:** `cinematic_promo`  ·  **Family:** Promo / Brand Film
**Derived from edits:** Sara Chy - FINAL

Polished branded story driven by music (no synced dialogue): graphic groups and animated text, layered color grade (RGB curves, levels, tint), masked composites and motion blur — a designed promo, not a talking edit.

**Best for:** Brand films, product/launch promos, event recaps, sizzles.

## Canvas

1920x1080 (4K source) · 16:9 · 30 fps

## Pacing

~80–110 cuts/min · avg clip 0.5–1.0s

Cut logic: **music_phrasing + montage**. 

## Reframing

Method: AE.ADBE Motion + Geometry · scale 100–140%

designed push/parallax moves

## Effect stack

- `AE.ADBE Graphic Group` — designed graphic layers
- `AE.ADBE Text` — animated brand typography
- `AE.ADBE RGB Curves` — primary grade
- `PR.ADBE Levels` — contrast/levels
- `AE.ADBE Tint` — brand tone
- `AE.ADBE AEMask` — masked composites / reveals
- `AE.ADBE Gaussian Blur 2` — depth / transitions
- `AE.ADBE Motion Blur` — movement realism
- `AE.ADBE Offset` — texture/parallax

## Transitions

_Choose the transition that fits the moment; never default to a hard cut._

**Default:** graphic/matte wipe

Library: graphic/matte wipe, blur dissolve on phrase, match cut, speed-ramp, cross dissolve

Transitions are designed moments — graphic wipes, match cuts and ramps cut to the music. Never an idle straight change.

## Audio

- **music:** drives the edit, full mix
- **dialogue:** none/VO optional
- **sync:** cut to phrasing + hits

## Color

- **approach:** strong cohesive brand grade
- **lumetri:** curves + levels + tint stack

## Titles & graphics

- **typography:** animated brand type
- **graphics:** designed lower-thirds / supers
- **logo:** outro lockup

## Toggles

`kinetic_typography`, `logo_outro`, `speed_ramps`, `texture_overlays`

## Generator rules

1. Build to music phrasing; place designed transitions on hits.
1. Layer graphic groups + animated type to a brand system.
1. Grade everything to one cohesive look; end on logo lockup.
